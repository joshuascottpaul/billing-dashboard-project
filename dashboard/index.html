Mobile-friendly reference
Top-1 Share: Percentage of annual invoice revenue coming from the largest customer in the focus year. Track this with Top-5 and Top-10 concentration. Persistent increases indicate dependency risk and potential volatility if one account slows or churns.
In simple terms: This shows how much you rely on your single biggest customer. If this keeps rising, your revenue is more exposed if that customer cuts spending.
Latest DSO: Days Sales Outstanding estimated from ending A/R versus rolling 3-month sales. This is a cash-conversion signal. Rising DSO can indicate slower customer payment behavior, disputes, or billing-to-collection process friction.
In simple terms: This is how long it takes to collect cash after invoicing. Higher numbers usually mean cash is coming in more slowly.
High-Risk A/R: Count of customers flagged with large or dominant 90+ day aging balances in the latest snapshot. Use this as collections triage and pair with customer drill-down before escalation.
In simple terms: This is the number of customers with overdue balances that may be hard to collect. Use it as your priority follow-up list.
Yearly Invoices, Payments, Credits: Annual view of gross invoicing, cash-in, and credit adjustments. If payments trail invoices for multiple years or credits spike, investigate contract changes, credit policy, or collection timing.
In simple terms: Compare what you billed, what you collected, and what you credited each year to see if money collection is keeping up.
Monthly Invoices + Forecast: Historical monthly invoices with optional baseline forecast. Forecast is directional planning support, not a committed target. Interpret alongside anomalies and customer concentration shifts.
In simple terms: This shows monthly billing trends and a forward estimate to help planning, but it is a guide, not a promise.
Concentration (Top 1/5/10): Annual revenue share captured by largest customers. If these lines rise while total revenue is flat, risk concentration is increasing even without top-line decline.
In simple terms: This tells you how dependent you are on a small group of big customers. Rising values mean more concentration risk.
Invoice Drift: Median and upper percentiles (p75/p90/p95) show ticket-size distribution movement. Rising p95 with flat median generally suggests large-deal mix expansion rather than broad base pricing change.
In simple terms: This shows whether invoice sizes are changing across your customer base. Big movement at the top often means more large deals.
Retention and Churn: New, retained, reactivated, and churned customers by year. Growth quality usually improves when retained and reactivated contributions rise sustainably relative to churn.
In simple terms: This shows how well you keep customers, win them back, and replace losses. Healthy growth usually means strong retention and controlled churn.
Unmatched Invoices: Reconciliation entries where unresolved balances remain under the matching model. Prioritize by amount and age; older unmatched balances are common indicators of dispute, process exception, or linkage gaps.
In simple terms: These are invoices that still do not tie out cleanly. Older and larger items should be resolved first because they often signal process or dispute issues.
